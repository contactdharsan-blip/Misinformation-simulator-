# Complete Research Bibliography: Misinformation Parameters

This document provides a comprehensive bibliography of research papers used to determine simulation parameters, organized by topic.

## Foundational Studies

### Spread Dynamics

1. **Vosoughi, S., Roy, D., & Aral, S. (2018).** The spread of true and false news online. *Science*, 359(6380), 1146-1151.
   - **DOI**: 10.1126/science.aap9559
   - **Key Parameters**: Spread rate ratio (6x), novelty, emotional content
   - **Access**: https://science.sciencemag.org/content/359/6380/1146
   - **Citations**: 2000+

2. **Goel, S., Anderson, A., Hofman, J., & Watts, D. J. (2016).** The structural virality of online diffusion. *Management Science*, 62(1), 180-196.
   - **DOI**: 10.1287/mnsc.2015.2158
   - **Key Parameters**: Structural virality (3.5), cascade patterns
   - **Access**: https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/abs/10.1287/mnsc.2015.2158

### Demographics and Sharing Behavior

3. **Guess, A., Nagler, J., & Tucker, J. (2019).** Less than you think: Prevalence and predictors of fake news dissemination on Facebook. *Science Advances*, 5(1), eaau4586.
   - **DOI**: 10.1126/sciadv.aau4586
   - **Key Parameters**: Share rate (8.5%), age ratio (7x), emotional drivers
   - **Access**: https://advances.sciencemag.org/content/5/1/eaau4586
   - **Citations**: 500+

4. **Guess, A. M., et al. (2018).** Exposure to untrustworthy websites in the 2016 US election. *Nature Human Behaviour*, 4(5), 472-480.
   - **DOI**: 10.1038/s41562-020-0833-x
   - **Key Parameters**: Exposure rates (25%), demographic patterns
   - **Access**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-020-0833-x

### Emotional and Cognitive Factors

5. **Brady, W. J., et al. (2020).** Emotion shapes the diffusion of moralized content in social networks. *PNAS*, 114(28), 7313-7318.

   - **Access**: https://www.pnas.org/content/114/28/7313

6. **Pennycook, G., & Rand, D. G. (2019).** Lazy, not biased: Susceptibility to partisan fake news is better explained by lack of reasoning than by motivated reasoning. *Cognition*, 188, 39-50.
   - **DOI**: 10.1016/j.cognition.2018.06.011

   - **Access**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S001002771830163X
